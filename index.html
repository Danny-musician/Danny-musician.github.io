Personal Bio

With over a decade of experience, I am a classically trained pianist and choir singer, as well as the former choir conductor for AiYue Chorus at BUPT. My musical journey also includes performing as a jazz and R&B vocalist with the NUS JazzBand (NJB).
My musical journey began at the tender age of four when I started learning classical piano, a passion that I pursued until I was 15. I was fortunate to study under the guidance of Mrs. Lü Zhen (吕真), an Associate Professor of Piano at the School of Music, South China Normal University, and a Visiting Professor at the Central Conservatory of Music. Her mentorship helped me build a solid foundation in classical music.
After my classical training, I ventured into the world of popular music by teaching myself pop piano arrangements. This exploration opened my eyes to the vast possibilities within music. In 2018, I expanded my repertoire by studying Chinese folk singing with Mrs. Zhang Xiuli (张秀丽), a graduate of the China Conservatory of Music.
During my undergraduate studies at Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT), I joined the AiYue Chorus as a conductor. I had the privilege of being guided by Mrs. Ma Menghe (马梦鹤) , Mr. Li Hong (李闳) , and Mr. Li Houpu (李厚朴) . At the same time, I sang as a bass in the Beijing Queer Chorus (BQC), which enriched my vocal experience.
In recent years, my musical interests have gravitated towards R&B, Soul, and Jazz. This passion led me to join the NUS Jazz Band as a vocalist, where I continually explore and perform these genres. Additionally, I perform some of my favorite pieces at our lab's (SMC) annual concert, sharing my love for music with a broader audience. Music has always been my way of communicating and expressing emotions. I relish the freedom of singing and the joy it brings to both myself and my listeners.
